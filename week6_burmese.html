Burmese Phonology Problem

Burmese is a Sino-Tibetan language of the Tibeto-Burman family, spoken in Myanmar. The following Burmese data contain both voiced and voiceless nasals. The latter are indicated by a small circle placed under the phonetic symbol in the original transcription.

Are [m] and [m̥] allophones of the same phoneme, or are they different phonemes? What about [n] and [n̥]? Is the same also true for [ɲ] and [ɲ̥]? Give evidence for your answer. If there is a phonological process involved, state what it is and give the conditioning environment.

Note: Burmese is a tone language. [á] = high tone [à] = low tone [â] = falling tone No tone marking indicates mid tone. The sequence [ei] is a diphthong.
